About

I am an energy data scientist leveraging Python to accelerate the transition to a sustainable energy system.

To better understand the world and the energy supply system fundamentally, I studied physics at RWTH Aachen University. During my master's, I researched electrochemistry in France, manufacturing and analyzing batteries in the Collège de France, Paris.

Back in Germany, I wrote my master thesis on the influence of weather patterns on energy system analysis and worked as a student employee in the team data science at Digital Energy Solutions.

Subsequently, I worked as a Ph.D. candidate at the Forschungszentrum Jülich, analyzing the influence of spatial aggregation on the computational complexity of energy system analysis.

Currently, I work as a software architect and data scientist at ACCURE Battery Intelligence GmbH, a startup I co-founded in 2020.

Along my path, Python has proven to be my language of choice and I am constantly developing my skills, recently as a mentor of the PyBites Developer Mindset Program.

You can contact me via email or on twitter, LinkedIn or GitHub.
